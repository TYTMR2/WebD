Goals


The Sustainable Development Goals were adopted by the United Nations in 2015 as a universal call to action to end poverty, protect the planet, and ensure that by 2030 all people enjoy peace and prosperity.

When specifically looking at Life Below Water our aims are:

Reducing Marine Pollution

By 2025, prevent and significantly reduce marine pollution of all kinds, in particular from land-based activities, including marine debris and nutrient pollution.

Resourceful fishing

By 2030, increase the economic benefits to Small Island developing States and least developed countries from the sustainable use of marine resources.

Changes to the law

Enhance the conservation and sustainable use of oceans and their resources by implementing international law as reflected in United Nations Convention on the Law of the Sea.

Reduce ocean acidification

Minimize and address the impacts of ocean acidification, including through enhanced scientific cooperation at all levels.

Increase scientific knowledge for ocean health.

Increase scientific knowledge, develop research capacity and transfer marine technology, taking into account the Intergovernmental Oceanographic Commission Criteria and Guidelines on the Transfer of Marine Technology.

Make a difference

  1. Find a Goal 14 charity you want to support. Any donation, big or small, can make a difference!
  2. Never buy bottled water – boil, filter, chlorine, rainwater, do what you can.
  3. Reduce waste – much of the waste that we produce on land ends up in the oceans. Stop using plastic bags: Usage and wrong disposal of plastic is a major cause of marine pollution.
  4. Run a campaign on the effects of plastic use on the seas and oceans.
  5. Organize a cleanup project for rivers and oceans. Engage your whole community to clean up a local river, seaside or an ocean.
  6. Buy local and certified fish. You can support small-scale producers by shopping in local markets and shops.
